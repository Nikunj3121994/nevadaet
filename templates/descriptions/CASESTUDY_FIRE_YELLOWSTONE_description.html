An unusually dry and warm summer and low winter snow accumulation in 1988 created ideal conditions for fires to ignite and burn. Most of the fires were started by lightning on lands adjacent to the park and, with high winds and dry conditions, quickly spread throughout the park. About 485,623 hectares (1.2 million acres) burned, and 36 percent of Yellowstone Park, or 320,916 of 899,131 hectares (793,000 of 2,221,800 acres).
