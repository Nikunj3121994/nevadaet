For much of Texas and Oklahoma, 2011 was the driest year on record. See this drought event depicted in many different ways below. Texas suffered an estimated $7.62 billion in crop and livestock losses, surpassing its record loss of $4.1 billion in 2006.

LST - MODIS Land Surface Temperature (LST) difference from the climatology from June - August 2011 shows increased surface temperatures due to reduced evapotranspiration and increased air temperatures.

NDVI - MODIS Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) difference from the climatology from June - August 2011 shows stressed vegetation.

PDSI - Palmer Drought Severity Index (PDSI) derived from gridMET precipitation, temperature, and ASCE Penman-Monteith grass reference ETo for April - September 2011 shows extreme drought conditions.
