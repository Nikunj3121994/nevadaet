Storm tracks that normally flow through the Central Great Plains during May and June were forced to the north and into Canada, which was followed by a lack of usual July and August thunderstorms. These conditions in combinations led to Dust Bowl era record breaking low spring and summer rainfall totals, and extreme drought conditions costing taxpayers upwards of $35 billion. See this drought event depicted in many different ways below.

LST - MODIS Land Surface Temperature (LST) difference from the climatology from May - August 2012 shows increased surface temperatures due to reduced evapotranspiration and increased air temperatures.
NDVI - MODIS Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) difference from the climatology from May - August 2012 shows stressed vegetation.
PDSI - Palmer Drought Severity Index (PDSI) derived from gridMET precipitation, temperature, and ASCE Penman-Monteith grass reference ETo for April - September 2012 shows severe drought conditions.
