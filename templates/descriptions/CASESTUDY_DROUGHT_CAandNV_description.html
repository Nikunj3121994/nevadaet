2014 was the driest and hottest on record in California, and many parts of Nevada experienced wet and cool summer conditions. See this drought depicted in many different ways below.

LST - MODIS Land Surface Temperature (LST) difference from the long term average (i.e. climatology) from July - September 2014. Results show high LST in California due to high air temperatures and low precipitation and evapotranspiration, and reduced irrigation. Low LST regions in Nevada are due to abnormally high summer precipitation, evapotranspiration, and humidity levels. Notice that major water bodies in Nevada (Pyramid Lake, Lake Tahoe, Walker Lake, Lake Mead) are 1 to 4 degrees C warmer than average.

PDSI - Summer Palmer Drought Severity Index (PDSI) derived from gridMET precipitation, temperature, and ASCE Penman-Monteith grass reference ETo for July - September 2014.

PDSI - Winter and growing season Drought Severity Index (PDSI) derived from gridMET precipitation, temperature, and ASCE Penman-Monteith grass reference ETo for January - September 2014.

NDVI - MODIS Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) difference from the climatology from July - September 2014 shows followed and stressed crops in the Central Valley an irrigated areas of western Nevada, fire scars in California and Nevada, and neutral to above normal vegetation greenness in central and eastern Nevada.
